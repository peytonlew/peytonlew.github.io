DAT301 Final Project¶

Braeden Burgard, Sabrina Vo & Peyton Lewis¶

We are analyzing what factors go into a good movie rating. Data comes from the rest API of omdp.

Movies chosen by https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_highest-grossing_films and https://www.imdb.com/list/ls055592025/. Movies have been selected through a combination of top grossing, classic, and random (for control) categories. What factors have led to the highest rated movies being so highly rated?

          "Fantasy","Musical","Romance","Sci-Fi","Thriller","War"]

#fill up the genreRatings arrays
for i in range(0,len(movies)):
    for genre in movieInfo.iloc[:,6][i].split(", "):
        #inside of try block, in case genre isn't in the list it won't fail
        try:
            genreRatings.loc['imdb',genre] = float(genreRatings.loc['imdb',genre]) + (float(ratings.iloc[i,0]) * 10)
            numGenreRatings.loc['imdb',genre] = float(numGenreRatings.loc['imdb',genre]) + 1
            genreRatings.loc['Metacritic',genre] = float(genreRatings.loc['Metacritic',genre]) + float(ratings.iloc[i,1])
            numGenreRatings.loc['Metacritic',genre] = float(numGenreRatings.loc['Metacritic',genre]) + 1
        except:
            x = "This is here for compilation only"
            
#calculate average genre ratings
for genre in genres:
    genreRatings.loc['imdb',genre] = float(genreRatings.loc['imdb',genre]) / float(numGenreRatings.loc['imdb',genre])
    genreRatings.loc['Metacritic',genre] = float(genreRatings.loc['Metacritic',genre]) / float(numGenreRatings.loc['Metacritic',genre])
